About TruthLens

Our Quest for Truth in an Age of Information Overload

Our Mission: Finding Truth, Even When It's Uncomfortable

In a world drowning in information, TruthLens exists for one purpose: to help you find the truth, even when it means adjusting your perspective.

We believe that truth-seeking requires intellectual honesty and humility. Sometimes the truth confirms what we believe. Sometimes it challenges us. Sometimes it surprises us entirely. Our job is to give you the tools to discover it—wherever it leads.

Truth doesn't care about our opinions. Our mission is to help you find it anyway.

Eight Tools for the Truth-Seeking Journey

TruthLens isn't just one app—it's a comprehensive ecosystem of eight interconnected tools designed to approach truth from multiple angles. Each tool serves a different purpose in your quest for clarity:

News Analysis

Evaluate news articles with 7 AI services analyzing credibility, bias, fact-checking, and more. Get a comprehensive Trust Score (0-100) and auto-extracted claims for verification.

Transcript Analysis

Fact-check speeches, interviews, YouTube videos, and transcripts. Extract claims, verify facts, assess credibility. Supports both text and video URL input.

AI Council

Ask any question to 10 different AI systems simultaneously. Compare GPT-4, Claude, Gemini, DeepSeek, Mistral, and more. See consensus, disagreements, and extracted claims.

AI Observatory

Monitor AI systems for existential threats. Track economic impacts, behavioral anomalies, and emergent capabilities across multiple AI platforms in real-time.

Media Literacy Quiz

Test your ability to spot misinformation, bias, and manipulation. Learn to identify logical fallacies, deepfakes, and deceptive framing through interactive challenges.

AI Bias Research

Comprehensive research tool comparing 8 AI systems across 20 categories and 1,250+ questions. Analyze political bias, cultural values, and alignment patterns with full CSV export.

Human Debates

Debate real people on any topic in structured rounds. Community voting determines winners. Democracy of ideas—may the best argument win.

AI Debates

Watch AI systems debate each other on any topic (3-15 rounds). Configure word limits, choose adversarial or truth-seeking mode. Optional AI judge scoring and MP3 audio generation.

How We Approach Truth

Truth is rarely found in a single source or perspective. That's why TruthLens uses multiple approaches:

  • Multiple AI Systems: We don't trust one AI—we compare many. Our AI Council queries 10 systems simultaneously. If they disagree, that tells you something important.
  • Consensus Analysis: When 7 different AI services agree on credibility or 10 AIs reach consensus on a claim, that's meaningful data.
  • Human Wisdom: AI is powerful, but humans provide context, nuance, and lived experience. Our debate and quiz tools combine both.
  • Transparency: We show you how we reach conclusions, not just what they are. You see individual AI responses, scoring breakdowns, and methodology.
  • Automated Verification: Claims are automatically extracted and added to a searchable database. Track what's been verified and what needs checking.
  • No Agenda: We're not here to tell you what to think. We're here to help you think better.
  • Continuous Improvement: Truth-seeking tools must evolve as deception techniques evolve. We're always adding new capabilities.

The goal isn't to be right—it's to get closer to truth. That means being willing to change your mind when evidence changes.

Why This Matters

We live in an era of unprecedented information access—and unprecedented misinformation. Deepfakes, AI-generated content, coordinated disinformation campaigns, and algorithmic echo chambers make it harder than ever to separate truth from fiction.

But here's the thing: the tools to deceive are getting better, so the tools to detect deception must get better too.

TruthLens is our contribution to that arms race. We're building tools that help you:

  • Verify what you read before you share it
  • Understand when you're being manipulated
  • See multiple perspectives instead of just one
  • Compare how different AI systems interpret the same information
  • Monitor AI developments for potential existential risks
  • Build media literacy skills through practice
  • Make informed decisions based on facts, not fear
  • Adjust your beliefs when new evidence emerges
